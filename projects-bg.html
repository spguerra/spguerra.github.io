Violão Brasileiro

Rogério Souza and Stephen Guerra

Like Spain with Flamenco, Brazil has its own tradition—an idiosyncratic performance practice, technique, and composition style—centered around the nylon-string guitar called Violão Brasileiro. This Brazilian Guitar Tradition begins with João Pernambuco in the late 1800s—contemporary and bandmate to the father of Brazilian music, Pixinguinha—and continues through such important composer-guitarists such as Dilermando Reis, Garoto, Canhoto da Paraíba, Baden Powell, Sebastião Tapajós, and Raphael Rabello, to the present day. The same tradition has also strongly influenced Brazil’s most well-known composers: Heitor Villa-Lobos, Antônio Carlos Jobim, and Radamés Gnattali.

In this project, Stephen teams up with his teacher, one of Brazil’s foremost 6- and 7-string guitarists continuing the tradition of instrumental choro and samba, Rogério Souza. Together, Rogério and Stephen present an innovative musical review of Brazil’s incredibly influential guitar tradition. Their programming and arrangements find a home in both chamber and world-music series, expanding knowledge in the US of what Brazilian music is, as well as concert repertoire for classical guitar duo.
