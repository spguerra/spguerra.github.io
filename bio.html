About Stephen

Stephen Guerra is a performer, teacher, and music theorist based in NYC.

Whether soloist or accompanist, Stephen’s playing style reflects his extensive study of classical and Brazilian music. He performs regularly in various projects or solo at music clubs and private events throughout the NYC tristate area, including Barbès, Cornelia Street Café, Bar Lunático, Jules Bistro, SOB’s, DROM, World Café Live, Rittenhouse Soundworks, and Best Video Film & Cultural Center.

Stephen teaches both individuals and classes. He has taught undergraduate music theory and aural skills at Yale and Temple Universities; he conceived and directed the ensemble-class Yale Choro Ensemble; he has given concert-workshops in Brazilian music at universities and community cultural centers; and he continues to give private guitar lessons.

When not performing or teaching it, Stephen likes to think about music abstractly. Having recently published his dissertation on meter, hemiola, and some recordings of master Afro-Brazilian guitarist Baden Powell, Stephen is now preparing a few articles intended for peer-reviewed journals. The working titles of these are “Expanded Meter, Hemiola, and Metric Space,” “Following the Changes, Changing the Meters: Harmonic Quantization in Brazilian Samba and Choro,” and “‘O patrono da bossa nova’: Reconstructing Moacir Santos’s Influence on Rhythmic Developments in Brazilian Popular Music Post-1960s.”

Stephen has worked professionally in business, the sciences, and the humanities and holds PhD, MPhil, and MA degrees from Yale University, a BM degree from Temple University, and a BAS degree from UPenn.
