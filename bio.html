About Stephen

Based out of New York City, Stephen Paul Guerra is a professional guitarist and music theorist. His repertoire includes Brazilian choro, samba, and art music as well as classical works.

He performs public and private concerts throughout the US Northeast as well as in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. He plays with some of Brazil’s leading instrumentalists, including Rogério Souza, Ronaldo do Bandolim, and Edinho Gerber, and gives undergraduate workshops in Brazilian guitar.

Stephen studied classical guitar with Allen Krantz and Brazilian guitar with Rogério Souza, Alessandro Penezzi, and Marcus Tardelli. Stephen has also studied Brazilian percussion and Flamenco guitar.

Stephen is a candidate for the Doctor of Philosophy degree in music theory from Yale University. Previously his work explored some of the more radical harmonic practices of the late 19th century. Currently, Stephen’s research exposes the richness of both popular and erudite Brazilian music as a source for new music-theoretical ideas. His dissertation builds a mathematical model of hemiola analytically applied to Brazilian guitarist Baden Powell’s instrumental samba recordings. Both the MacMillan Foundation and the Council on Latin American and Iberian Studies have generously supported his work.

Stephen holds Master of Philosophy and Master of Arts degrees from Yale University, a Bachelor of Music degree from Temple University, and a Bachelor of Applied Science from the University of Pennsylvania. Richard Cohn and Michael Klein have been his principal academic mentors.
