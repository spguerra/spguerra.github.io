About Stephen

Performer, pedagogue, and music theorist Stephen Guerra performs regularly in various Brazilian ensembles throughout the NYC area. Stephen’s musical approach, attack, and ideas reveal both his classical training as well as the heavy influence of Brazilian guitarists Baden Powell and Rogério Souza.

Stephen has worked in business, the sciences, and the humanities. Stephen has taught music at Temple and Yale Universities and regularly gives concert-workshops in Brazilian music. In between performances Stephen is finishing a dissertation on rhythm, meter, and hemiola in the music of Baden Powell.

Stephen is a PhD candidate (expected 2018) at Yale University. Both the MacMillan Foundation and the Council on Latin American and Iberian Studies have generously supported his research. Stephen also holds Master of Philosophy and Master of Arts degrees from Yale University, a Bachelor of Music degree from Temple University, and a Bachelor of Applied Science from the University of Pennsylvania.
